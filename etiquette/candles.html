How to light candles (and prayers)

A candle is a small offering and a prayer before the Lord or a saint.

At a glance

  • Light the candle calmly, place it upright before the appropriate icon.
  • Use short prayers: “Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, have mercy on me,” “Most Holy Theotokos, save us,” “Saint N., pray to God for us.”
  • During services, candles on stands follow the set order; don’t interfere with it.
  • Holding candles: Great Feasts (specific points), Great and Holy Thursday (Twelve Gospels), Pascha (from the procession), and funerals.
  • Don’t block others, don’t rearrange others’ candles, keep wax drips minimal.

Short prayers before icons

  • Before Christ: “Lord Jesus Christ, Son of God, have mercy on me, a sinner.”
  • Before the Mother of God: “Most Holy Theotokos, save us.”
  • Before the Cross: “Glory, O Lord, to Your honorable Cross.”
  • Before a Guardian Angel: “Angel of Christ, my holy guardian, save me.”
  • Before a Saint: “Saint N., pray to God for us.”
  • At the memorial table: “Grant rest, O Lord, to Your departed servants.”

Where and how

  1. Approach the icon stand calmly.
  2. Make the sign of the Cross, bow, and light the candle from an existing flame if possible.
  3. Place it upright before the icon of Christ, the Mother of God, or the saint you are addressing.
  4. Do not block others, jostle, or rearrange others’ candles. Keep wax drips to a minimum.

When candles on stands are lit during services

At Vespers, candles are lit before censing at “Lord, I have cried,” then follow the set pattern of being extinguished and relit at specific moments, remaining lit through the Great Doxology. At the morning service and the Divine Liturgy, candles are lit after the Ninth Hour and remain lit until the end. Don’t wander around tending candles in a way that disrupts the service or decorum.

When the faithful hold candles

  • Great Feasts (Twelve Great Feasts and similar rank, and the parish patronal feast): from the Polyeleos until the 3rd Ode of the Canon; extinguish, then relight after the 8th Ode until the end of the Great Doxology.
  • Great and Holy Thursday: during the reading of the Twelve Gospels.
  • Pascha: from the procession until the exchange of the Paschal greeting (end of the First Hour).
  • Funerals and burial services.

Conduct

Offer your candle without disrupting worship. Respect the order of the service and the people around you so that a simple offering becomes a fitting prayer.
